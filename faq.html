Frequently Asked Questions




  • What is XR? arrow_drop_down

    XR stands for Extended Reality and it is a catch-all term for technologies that blend together the real and virtual worlds such as virtual reality(VR), augmented reality(AR), and mixed reality(MR). The 'X' is XR represents any letter of the alphabet which may be used for technology in the future.

  • What are examples of XR technology? arrow_drop_down

    Considering XR is the umbrella term for VR, AR, and MR, there are many applications of this technology today. VR is used in videos games such as Half-Life: Alyx, in healthcare to help patients with pain relief and rehabilitation, and in virtual tourism to allow users to 'step in' to a destination across the world. Examples of AR include mobile games such as Pokemon Go, the Ikea Place app, and Snapchat lenses. AR blends digiral information over a live camera view. Mixed Reality examples include the Microsoft Hololens, Magic Leap, and select smartphone apps.

  • How does XR technology collect data? arrow_drop_down

    XR technologies have many different sensors that collect data about their users. These include location data, body movements, gaze tracking, respiration and heart activity, brain activity, audio, video, and usage. The large amount of data collected has raised concerns for users' privacy and security. It is virtually impossible to maintain anonymity with XR technologies.

  • How would XR help me or my company? arrow_drop_down

    XR can create an immersive experience like never before that is great for communication, and can present your ideas/designs clearly to clients and colleagues in a way that hasn't been done before. Whether you're trying to explore designs that are not completed, XR allows you to explore the full capabilities before the product is even made.

  • Why should I design with XR? arrow_drop_down

    XR is a powerful tool that can make designing in new ways that were never possible before. No other tool is the world has quite the experience XR and helps visioning a product before its actually made. Collaborators on the project can work within, moving/changing the design however they want, very easily. This makes group productivity better than ever.
